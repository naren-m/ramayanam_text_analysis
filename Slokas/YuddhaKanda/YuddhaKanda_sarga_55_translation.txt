6::55::1::On hearing about Vajradamshtra being killed by Vali's son, Ravana spoke to the chief of the army who stood with folded hands.
6::55::2::Send for Akampana quickly, who is of terrific valour, difficult to encounter and skilled in all sastras.
6::55::3::Akampana is a tamer of foes, protector of people, in that way capable, always desirous of welfare, lover of war.
6::55::4::Scorcher of enemies, this Akampana will win Sugriva of terrific might, Rama and Lakshmana, dreadful Vanaras, and put an end to enemies.
6::55::5::Then accepting Ravana's order, accordingly, taking hold of the army dispatched them.
6::55::6::Thereafter taking different weapons, Rakshasas of fearful eyes and terrific appearance rushed forward ordered by CommanderinChief of the army.
6::55::7::Mounted on a huge chariot, Akampana, decked in pure gold jewels, like cloud in form and complexion, whose loud voice resembled thunder, surrounded by Rakshasas went forward.
6::55::8::Akampana who, possibly could not be shaken in war by suras shone like the sun in that manner.
6::55::9::As Akampana was going forth excited to wage war, an expression of desperation developed suddenly in the horses drawing the chariots and an experience of depression in him.
6::55::10::Himself being a lover of war, became pale with his countenance changed, voice choked, and his eyes lost its lustre.
6::55::11::At a time on a good day of fine weather, it became cloudy, birds and beasts were making frightening cries all over.
6::55::12::He whose shoulders were developed like that of a lion, who equalled tiger in valour, went forth not minding the evil portents.
6::55::13::While that Akampana was going like that along with Rakshasas huge noise that arose was as if the ocean was agitated.
6::55::14::Huge troops of Vanaras holding trees and hillocks stood prepared for waging and by that the noise that arose expanded.
6::55::15::Vanaras and Rakshasas dedicated their lives for Rama and Ravana, respectively in that way were enraged and battle took place.
6::55::16::Intending to kill one another both Vanaras and Rakshasas who resembled hills, also of extraordinary might and heroic fighting.
6::55::17::A tremendous sound thundered in that war when they fought with exceeding impetuosity with one another and yelled at each other.
6::55::18::Terrific dust of red colour arose instantly in all the ten directions by the Vanaras, and Rakshasas and it enveloped all over.
6::55::19::The combatants could not see one another in the battlefield, and it seemed as though white silk piece was shaken by the wind by the appearance of the surrounding dust.
6::55::20::By the dust raised, the posts, flags, shields and not even horses, weapons and chariots were visible.
6::55::21::As they were rushing over one another, terrific noise was heard, and no form was discernible.
6::55::22::Then in that darkness and in anger Vanaras went attacking Vanaras and Rakshasas also attacked Rakshasas (not knowing).
6::55::23::Not knowing enemies and own people the Vanaras and Rakshasas terminating drenched the mud with their blood and covered the earth.
6::55::24::Then moistened with blood, the dust settled on the ground was seen covered with bodies and limbs.
6::55::25::Then Rakshasas and Vanaras fought one another vigorously with trees, javelins, maces, darts, rocks, and peaks.
6::55::26::The Vanaras of terrific deeds, like hills in form, resembling iron bars attacked many Rakshasas in the battle.
6::55::27::There, the highly furious Rakshasas also exterminated the Vanaras with their weapons and darts.
6::55::28::Highly enraged Akampana of fierce valour cheered all the Rakshasa troops.
6::55::29::Even the Vanaras mangled Rakshasas countering with weapons, huge rocks and trees and destroyed them.
